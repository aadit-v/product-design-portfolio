01 — Problem
Problem
The Gravity Cutter is designed with three main problems regarding the traditional box/board cutter in mind: the groove for moving the blade on one side which digs into the user's cutting hand during repetitive cutting hurting the hand in the process, the straight handle needing to be held at an angle which creates extreme radial deviation hurting the wrist, and the issue of safety caused by leaving the cutter blade exposed.

03 — Solution
Solution
Designed specifically for sustained usage with a special focus towards ergonomics, the Gravity Cutter transitions seamlessly from closed to cutting-ready in one fluid motion. Tailored to be used for model making by architects, interior designers and industrial designers.

04 — Mechanical Details
Mechanical Details
The Gravity Cutter uses the cutting motion combined with the weight of the blade and slider to automatically lock the blade in position. The lock remains engaged until the cutter is turned upside down, at which point the blade is released and can be retracted. This design eliminates the need for a manual locking step — the blade opens and locks through gravity alone.
